🎯 Must-Know Quant Formulas (Rank-Maker High ROI)

Percentage & Product Constancy

If value increases by $x\%$, decrease needed to restore original = $\frac{x}{100+x} \times 100\%$. Fraction equivalents: $\frac{1}{6} \approx 16.67\%$, $\frac{1}{7} \approx 14.28\%$, $\frac{1}{8} \approx 12.5\%$, $\frac{1}{9} \approx 11.11\%$, $\frac{1}{11} \approx 9.09\%$, $\frac{1}{12} \approx 8.33\%$.

Profit, Loss & Successive Discounts

Relation: $\frac{\text{Cost Price (CP)}}{\text{Marked Price (MP)}} = \frac{100 - d\%}{100 + p\%}$. Successive discounts of $d_1\%$ and $d_2\% = (d_1 + d_2 - \frac{d_1 d_2}{100})\%$. Faulty weight: $\text{Profit \%} = \frac{\text{Error}}{\text{True Value} - \text{Error}} \times 100\%$.

Simple & Compound Interest Differences

Difference for 2 Years: $\text{Diff} = \frac{P \times R^2}{100^2}$. For 3 Years: $\text{Diff} = \frac{P \times R^2 \times (300 + R)}{100^3}$. Equivalent rate of CI for 2 Years at $R\% = 2R + \frac{R^2}{100}\%$. Installment (CI): $P = \frac{I}{1+R/100} + \frac{I}{(1+R/100)^2}$.

Algebraic Identities & Roots

$a^3+b^3+c^3 - 3abc = (a+b+c)(a^2+b^2+c^2 - ab - bc - ca) = \frac{1}{2}(a+b+c)[(a-b)^2 + (b-c)^2 + (c-a)^2]$. If $a+b+c=0$, then $a^3+b^3+c^3 = 3abc$. Reciprocal: If $x + \frac{1}{x} = k$, then $x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2} = k^2 - 2$, and $x^3 + \frac{1}{x^3} = k^3 - 3k$.

Geometry Centers & Circle Theorems

Inradius of right triangle $r = \frac{P+B-H}{2}$. Circumradius $R = \frac{H}{2}$. Tangent Secant Theorem: $PT^2 = PA \times PB$. Alternate Segment Theorem: Angle between chord and tangent equals angle subtended by chord in alternate segment. Centroid divides median in $2:1$. Apollonius Theorem: $AB^2 + AC^2 = 2(AD^2 + BD^2)$ where $AD$ is median.

Mensuration (3D Surfaces & Volumes)

Frustum of Cone: Volume $= \frac{1}{3}\pi h(R^2 + r^2 + Rr)$, Curved Area $= \pi l(R+r)$ where $l = \sqrt{h^2 + (R-r)^2}$. Sphere: Volume $= \frac{4}{3}\pi R^3$, Area $= 4\pi R^2$. Pyramid: Volume $= \frac{1}{3} \times \text{Base Area} \times \text{Height}$, Lateral Area $= \frac{1}{2} \times \text{Base Perimeter} \times \text{Slant Height}$.

Time, Speed, Distance & Boats

Average speed for same distances = $\frac{2xy}{x+y}$. Relative speed: opposite direction $= S_1 + S_2$, same direction $= |S_1 - S_2|$. Upstream Boat Speed $= U - V$, Downstream Speed $= U + V$, where $U$ is boat speed in still water and $V$ is stream speed. Distance formula: $D = \text{Speed Difference} \times \frac{T_1 \times T_2}{T_1 - T_2}$ or $D = \frac{S_1 S_2}{S_1 - S_2} \times \text{Time Diff}$.

Time & Work Equivalence

Formula: $M_1 D_1 H_1 E_1 / W_1 = M_2 D_2 H_2 E_2 / W_2$ (Men, Days, Hours, Efficiency, Work). If A does work in $x$ days and B in $y$ days, together they take $\frac{xy}{x+y}$ days. Negative work (Pipes & Cistern): Leak draining capacity is subtracted from inlet capacity.

Trigonometric Ratios & Height Angles

Ratios: 30°-60°-90° $\rightarrow 1 : \sqrt{3} : 2$; 45°-45°-90° $\rightarrow 1 : 1 : \sqrt{2}$. Identities: $\sin(A+B) = \sin A\cos B + \cos A\sin B$, $\cos(A+B) = \cos A\cos B - \sin A\sin B$. Max value of $a\sin\theta + b\cos\theta = \sqrt{a^2+b^2}$, Min value $= -\sqrt{a^2+b^2}$. If $A+B = 90^\circ$, then $\tan A\tan B = 1$ and $\sin^2 A + \sin^2 B = 1$.

Coordinate Geometry

Distance between $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2) = \sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2 + (y_2-y_1)^2}$. Section formula (internal division): $(x, y) = (\frac{m x_2 + n x_1}{m+n}, \frac{m y_2 + n y_1}{m+n})$. Slope of line $m = \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}$. If parallel: $m_1 = m_2$; if perpendicular: $m_1 m_2 = -1$. Area of triangle $= \frac{1}{2}|x_1(y_2-y_3) + x_2(y_3-y_1) + x_3(y_1-y_2)|$.

✍️ English Grammar Rules (Error Spotting High ROI)

Subject-Verb Agreement Core Cases

If two subjects are joined by *along with, together with, in addition to, as well as, accompanied by*, the verb agrees with the **First Subject**. If joined by *neither... nor, either... or, not only... but also*, the verb agrees with the **Nearest Subject**. Singular verbs follow expressions like *each of, either of, neither of, one of* + plural noun (e.g., "Each of the boys has *[not have]* completed his work").

Conditional Clauses Structure

Type 1: If + Present Simple, Will/Can + V1 (Real future possibility). Type 2: If + Past Simple, Would + V1 (Imaginary present). Type 3: If + Past Perfect, Would have + V3 (Past unfulfilled action). *Crucial*: In imaginary conditions, use "were" regardless of subject (e.g., "If I *were* a king, I would help the poor"). Do not use "will" or "would" inside the *if-clause*.

Active / Passive Transformations

Passive of imperative sentence "Do it" = "Let it be done" or "You are ordered to do it." Modals: "She can play cricket" $\rightarrow$ "Cricket can be played by her." Prepositions do not vanish: "He laughed at me" $\rightarrow$ "I was laughed at by him." For transitive verbs only. Interrogative active: "Who did this?" $\rightarrow$ "By whom was this done?"

Direct & Indirect Speech Rules

Tense shifts (if reporting verb is in past): Present Simple $\rightarrow$ Past Simple; Present Perfect $\rightarrow$ Past Perfect; Past Simple $\rightarrow$ Past Perfect. Pronoun shift formula: **SON** (1st person subject, 2nd person object, 3rd person no change). Questions: Reporting verb "said to" changes to "asked". No conjunction "that" is used for wh-questions; use "if/whether" for yes/no questions.

Plural vs Singular Noun Traps

Plural in form, singular in use: *Mathematics, Physics, News, Politics, Billiards, Gallows, Innings* (always take a singular verb). Singular in form, plural in use: *Cattle, Gentry, Clergy, Poultry, Peasantry, People, Police* (always take a plural verb; e.g., "Cattle are grazing *[not is grazing]*"). Uncountable nouns: *Scenery, Furniture, Advice, Information, Luggage, Hair* (never take plural forms or "a/an").

Pronoun Order & Cases

When multiple persons are in one sentence, normal order is **Second Person (2) $\rightarrow$ Third Person (3) $\rightarrow$ First Person (1)** (e.g., "You, he and I are study partners"). If confessing a mistake or guilt, the order becomes **1 $\rightarrow$ 2 $\rightarrow$ 3** (e.g., "I, you and he committed the error"). Objective case follow prepositions and "let" (e.g., "Between you and me *[not I]*", "Let him and me *[not I]* go").

Fixed Prepositions Combinations

*Abstain from* (not of) • *Proficient in* • *Accused of* • *Comply with* • *Different from* (not than) • *Congratulate on* (not for) • *Refrain from* • *Prevent from* • *Key to* success • *Aim at* • *Deprived of* • *Adapted to* environment.

🧠 General Intelligence & Reasoning Tricks

Alphabet Code Map (EJOTY)

Memorize position blocks: E(5), J(10), O(15), T(20), Y(25). Opposite alphabet pairs sum is always **27** (A+Z = 27, B+Y = 27). Mnemonic reverse pairs: A-Z (Azhar), B-Y (Boy), C-X (Crux), D-W (Dew), E-V (Love/Even), G-T (G.T. Road), H-S (High School), I-R (Indian Railways), K-P (K.P. Thakur), L-O (Love), M-N (Man).

Clock Hands Angle Equation

Angle between Hour hand and Minute hand $= |30H - \frac{11}{2}M|$ degrees. In 1 hour, hour hand travels 30° ($0.5^\circ$ per minute) and minute hand travels 360° ($6^\circ$ per minute). Relative speed is $5.5^\circ$ per minute. Hands coincide every $65\frac{5}{11}$ minutes.

Calendar Odd Days Rules

Normal year $= 365 \text{ days} = 52 \text{ weeks} + 1 \text{ odd day}$. Leap year $= 366 \text{ days} = 52 \text{ weeks} + 2 \text{ odd days}$. Centenary leap years must be divisible by 400 (1600, 2000 are leap; 1700, 1800, 1900 are normal). Month codes for ordinary year: Jan(0), Feb(3), Mar(3), Apr(6), May(1), Jun(4), Jul(6), Aug(2), Sep(5), Oct(0), Nov(3), Dec(5).

Syllogism Truth Matrix

"Only a few A are B" implies: **Some A are B** AND **Some A are NOT B**. Possibility cases: A conclusion stating "All A can be B is a possibility" is false (since some A must not be B), but "All B can be A is a possibility" is true.

Direction Sense Shadows & Triplets

At sunrise, shadow is always towards **West**. At sunset, shadow is always towards **East**. No shadow at 12:00 Noon. Shortcuts: Memorize Pythagorean triplets to find shortest distance instantly: (3,4,5), (5,12,13), (8,15,17), (7,24,25), (9,40,41), (20,21,29).

Dice Closed & Open Rules

Standard Dice: Sum of opposite faces is always **7** (1 opposite 6, 2 opposite 5, 3 opposite 4). Ordinary Dice: Sum of adjacent faces can be 7. Closed Dice Rule: If two positions of a single dice have one common face in the same position, then the remaining faces in the same positions are opposite to each other.
